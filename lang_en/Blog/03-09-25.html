Recruiting profiles: agility, precision, and talent ready to make an impact

Are you looking to add IT or finance professionals to your team and encounter slow processes, poorly qualified candidates, or delayed decisions? You're not alone.

Specialized recruitment has become a real bottleneck for many companies, especially when internal teams lack the focus or resources to address it with the necessary speed and depth. In this context, an increasingly adopted solution is emerging: strategic staffing.

What is outsourcing staffing?

Staffing is a model for providing professional talent where a specialized provider (such as Novatium) selects, manages, and provides qualified profiles, either to fill temporary positions or to join longer-term projects.

This model allows companies to:

  • Reducesearch times
  • Access validated and productive talentfrom day one
  • Scale up or down teams with agility
  • Avoid the operational and contractual burden of direct contracting

In other words, staffing staffing isn't just about filling a vacancy, it's about ensuring that talent boosts the business from the very beginning. How do we do this at Novatium?

At Novatium, we design our own strategic staffing model that combines speed and quality, tailored to each client's specific context. Our approach includes:

  • AI to filter candidates based on technical profile and cultural fit
  • Automated technical testing en desarrollo, cloud, ciberseguridad, ERP, finanzas y más
  • Soft skills assessments based on the Big Ocean Model, which predict performance in high-demand environments
  • Delivery of validated shortlist : we only present candidates ready for a second interview

In addition, we cover needs as diverse as:

  • Freelancers for short projects (1 to 3 months)
  • Full-time technical staff under a long-term scheme
  • Critical equipment escalation or replacement in 1 week

The new skills that matter

In addition to technical competencies, today's talent must master skills that are no longer optional:

  • Autonomy and accountability
  • Critical thinking and data orientation
  • Time management in distributed environments
  • Effective communication and problem solving
  • Continuous learning and digital flexibility
